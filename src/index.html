About me

I study Computer Science and General Business at the University of Virginia, pursuing my passion for learning how to create software that solves real-world challenges and drives long-term impact.

Outside of class, I study Human-Robot Interaction as a Research Assistant for the UVa Engineering Link Lab - Collaborative Robotics Lab - and a MATH 1310 (Calculus 1) Peer Coach for the UVa P2L Program.

In my free time, I enjoy hiking, squash, reading, scrapbooking, and refining my mahjong strategy. I'm naturally curious, and as such I often find myself exploring new topics outside the scope of my coursework. I love challenging myself with unique goals and adventures, and I'm currently training for my first ski-mountaineering race in February.

My goal as a software engineer is to design technology that transforms our current solution set for complex world problems driven by a focus on users and the direct and indirect impact of deployment.
